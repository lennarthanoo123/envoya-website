1 Data Controller
1.1
The data controller for the processing activities described in this Privacy Policy is Propio Negocio B.V., trading as Envoya, Bronsbergen 22, 7207 AA Zutphen, the Netherlands. You can reach us at admin@envoya.tech.
2 What Personal Data We Collect
2.1
Website visitors. When you visit envoya.tech, we may collect: your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URL, pages viewed, and time of visit. This data is collected through server logs and, where applicable, analytics cookies (see Section 7).
2.2
Contact and enquiry data. When you contact us via email, a contact form, or schedule a demo, we collect: your name, email address, company name, phone number (if provided), and the content of your message.
2.3
Client data. When you become a client, we additionally collect: company name, billing address, Chamber of Commerce number, VAT number, bank details (for invoicing), and names and email addresses of Authorized Users as defined in our General Terms and Conditions.
2.4
Lead Machine usage. The Lead Machine software runs locally on hardware at the client's premises. Envoya does not collect, store, or have access to any campaign data, prospect lists, ICP configurations, or conversation histories generated through the Software, except when the client explicitly grants remote access via Tailscale for support purposes. During such support sessions, any data accessed is used solely for troubleshooting and is not retained by Envoya.
3 Purposes and Legal Bases
3.1
We process your personal data for the following purposes and on the following legal bases under Article 6(1) of the GDPR:

(a) Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): processing client contact and billing data to deliver our products and services, issue invoices, provide onboarding and support, and administer the contractual relationship.

(b) Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR): analysing website traffic to improve our website and services; responding to enquiries from prospective clients; preventing fraud and ensuring security.

(c) Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR): placing non-essential cookies on your device (see Section 7); sending marketing communications where you have opted in. You may withdraw consent at any time.

(d) Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR): retaining invoicing and financial records as required by Dutch tax law.
4 Third-Party Services and Data Transfers
4.1
We may share personal data with the following categories of recipients, solely to the extent necessary for the purposes described in Section 3: hosting and infrastructure providers; email service providers; payment processors; accountancy and legal advisors.
4.2
Our website may use analytics tools (such as Plausible Analytics or similar privacy-friendly alternatives). Where analytics are used, we favour services that do not place tracking cookies and that process data within the EEA.
4.3
The Lead Machine software integrates with third-party External Services including Apollo.io, Anthropic API, Brave Search API, and Tailscale. These services are contracted and managed by the client directly. Data transmitted to these services is governed by the respective provider's own privacy policy and terms. Envoya is not a controller or processor in respect of data processed through these External Services by the client.
4.4
We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for their marketing purposes.
4.5
Where personal data is transferred outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) adopted by the European Commission, or a European Commission adequacy decision for the recipient country.
5 Data Retention
5.1
We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected:

(a) Website analytics data: aggregated and anonymised; raw server logs are deleted within 90 days.

(b) Contact and enquiry data: retained for up to 12 months after our last interaction, unless a contractual relationship is established.

(c) Client data: retained for the duration of the contractual relationship and for 7 years thereafter to comply with Dutch tax and accounting obligations.
5.2
After the applicable retention period, personal data is securely deleted or anonymised.
6 Data Security
6.1
We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include encryption of data in transit (TLS), access controls, and regular review of our security practices.
6.2
While we take reasonable precautions, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.
7 Cookies
7.1
Our website may use cookies and similar technologies. A cookie is a small text file placed on your device when you visit a website.
7.2
Essential cookies. We may use strictly necessary cookies to ensure basic website functionality (e.g. session management). These do not require consent.
7.3
Analytics cookies. If we use analytics cookies, we will ask for your consent before placing them. You can withdraw consent at any time through your browser settings or through any cookie consent mechanism on our website.
7.4
We do not use advertising or tracking cookies.
8 Your Rights
8.1
Under the GDPR, you have the following rights with respect to your personal data:
  • Right of access — you may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification — you may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure — you may request deletion of your data where there is no compelling reason for continued processing.
  • Right to restrict processing — you may request that we limit the way we use your data.
  • Right to data portability — you may request a machine-readable copy of data you provided to us.
  • Right to object — you may object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.
8.2
To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at admin@envoya.tech. We will respond within one (1) month of receiving your request. If your request is complex, we may extend this period by up to two (2) additional months, in which case we will inform you of the extension and the reasons for it.
8.3
If you believe that we have not handled your personal data in accordance with applicable law, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens), Bezuidenhoutseweg 30, 2594 AV The Hague, the Netherlands (autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl).
9 Children
9.1
Our website and services are not directed at individuals under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a child, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.
10 Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The most current version will always be available at envoya.tech/privacy. Where changes are material, we will notify clients by email. Your continued use of our website or services after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.
